Wedding Invitation Site Plan

Site Name & Logo Justification

Site Name: Shirley & Gilberto: The Next Chapter

The name is highly relevant and specific to the project's purpose. It uses the couple's names for immediate recognition and the subtitle ("The Next Chapter") reflects the romantic theme of celebrating the start of their new life together.

Logo/Primary Identifier:

A charming circular crest featuring the full names 'Shirley and Gilberto' in an elegant script, framed by intertwining gold vines and muted rose accents. The logo perfectly establishes the romantic and elegant visual theme for the site's branding.

Site Purpose

The site serves as a comprehensive digital invitation and information hub for the wedding of Shirley and Gilberto. Its primary scope is to facilitate efficient guest management (RSVP collection) and provide all necessary logistical and gift registry information to the target audience.

Scenarios

These questions represent the target audience's primary needs and drive the required content.

Scenario Page that provides the answer
We need to book a hotel. What accommodations are recommended and where is the venue address? Details (Venue & Logistics)
Where is the couple registered, and what if I want to contribute to their honeymoon fund? Registry (Gifts)
What is the final deadline to submit my attendance and my meal preference? RSVP (Form)

Color Schema

The site uses an elegant, soft, and romantic palette.

Primary: Muted Rose/Blush

#8C485A

Accent: Soft Gold

#B28F50

Background: Creamy Off-White

#F7F5F0
Color Usage
Muted Rose/Blush (#8C485A) Major Headings (H1, H3), Footer Background, Monogram Border, Primary Text.
Soft Gold (#B28F50) Accent Headings (H2), Monogram Fill, Call-to-Action Buttons, Link Hover States, Borders/Separators.
Creamy Off-White (#F7F5F0) Primary Background Color (Body).

Typography

A combination of an elegant serif font for display text and a clean sans-serif for high readability.

Playfair Display (Serif)

Usage: Primary Headings (H1, H2, H3) and Hero Text.

Lato (Sans-Serif)

Usage: Body Text, Navigation, Form Labels, and General Paragraphs.
